How the Math Works

Everything that decides a pool — payouts, parlay bonuses, and tiebreakers — is published here so the standings are fully auditable. ← Back

1. Two pool formats

Horse racing pools come in two flavors depending on which event you're looking at:

  • Allocation pool (Preakness onward — the new default) — every player gets a fixed bankroll (e.g. $5,000) to split across 5 bets however they want, subject to minimums and maximums.
  • Fixed-stake pool (Derby 2026 — legacy) — every pick has the same fixed stake; players add a parlay lock for bonus action.

The rest of this page covers both. If you're here for Preakness, skip to section 2. If you're here for Derby, jump to section 4.

2. Allocation pool — Preakness format

You get a fixed bankroll (default $5,000) to allocate across 5 bets. Each bet must have between $250 and $2,000 and the five must sum to exactly $5,000. Adjust with the +/− buttons on the public page.

The 5 bets and their payoffs

Each payoff formula returns your stake plus the multiplied profit. Miss the bet → $0 (lose the stake).

Top 5 scoring detail (the gradient bet)

The Top 5 bet rewards both spread (more correct horses = bigger multiplier) and conviction on longshots (a 25/1 horse hitting adds 25 to the multiplier, vs. 5 for a 5/1).

multiplier = (# correct picks) + (sum of decimal odds of correct picks)

Example at $1,000 stake, actual top 5 = [#19, #1, #22, #12, #6]:

Picks landedOdds of hitsMultiplierPayout
1 of 5 (just #1)5/11 + 5 = 6$7,000
3 of 55/1, 25/1, 20/13 + 50 = 53$54,000
5 of 5full mix5 + sum of all 5often $50K–$100K+

3. Allocation pool — tiebreaker

If two or more players tie on final bankroll:

  1. Total $ staked on winning bets — whoever put more money on the bets that hit wins. Rewards conviction.
  2. Name alphabetical — final fallback, so the system is always deterministic.

4. Fixed-stake pool — Derby format (legacy)

Every pick has a fixed stake (e.g. $1,000). Payoff depends on the pick type:

Pick typePayoff if hitExample at $1,000 stake
Win / Place / Showstake × decimal odds (morning line)$1,000 on a 6/1 horse → $6,000
Trifecta (1-2-3 in order)50 × stake$50,000 if exact
Top-3 Box (any order)10 × stake$10,000 if all 3 in top 3
Longshot top-35 × stake$5,000 bonus
Over/Under, Yes/No propseven money (1 × stake)$1,000

Odds are pari-mutuel — payouts use the odds at race time (last imported), not when you locked in your pick. Same as the actual track.

Parlay bonus (fixed-stake only)

You can lock 2 or 3 of your single-horse picks (Win, Place, Show) into a parlay:

bonus = stake × (odds₁ × odds₂ × odds₃)

Example: lock Win at 8/1 and Place at 5/2. Both hit → bonus = 1,000 × 8 × 2.5 = $20,000. Miss any leg → bonus is zero (individual picks still pay normally).

5. Fixed-stake — tiebreaker ladder (Derby)

If two or more players tie on bankroll, the tiebreaker cascade kicks in. Each tier resolves first; if still tied, falls through to the next tier.

Tier 1 — Trifecta position error (lowest wins)

For each of your 3 trifecta picks: error = |slot − actual_finish_position|. Slot is 1, 2, or 3. Scratched/DNF horses get a 20-point penalty. Sum the three errors.

Tier 2 — Exact position hits (most wins)

Count how many of your 3 trifecta picks finished in the exact position you predicted. Range 0–3.

Tier 3 — Exacta error (lowest wins)

Same as Tier 1 but only for your first two trifecta picks.

Tier 4 — Coin flip

If still tied after all three tiers, admin makes a manual call.

7. Audit seal

Just before the race lock, the admin "seals" all entries. We compute a SHA-256 hash of the entry snapshot (canonical JSON, sorted keys, no timestamps) and publish it. Anyone can click Verify integrity on the event page to re-fetch the snapshot, recompute the hash, and confirm nothing was changed after seal time.

If picks were tampered with after sealing, the hash wouldn't match — verify would fail. The proof requires no trust in the platform or the admin.
