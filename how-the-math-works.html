How the Math Works

Everything that decides a pool — payouts, parlay bonuses, and tiebreakers — is published here so the standings are fully auditable. ← Back

1. The pool format

Every player gets a fixed bankroll (default $5,000) to allocate across 6 bets. Each bet must be between $250 and $2,000 and the six must sum to exactly $5,000. Use the +/− buttons on the public page to adjust.

Highest final bankroll wins the pool. Ties are broken by the cascade in section 3.

2. The bets and their payoffs

TrifectaPick the top 3 in exact order. Hardest bet on the slip; pays the headline reward.stake × 12 + stake
Long Shot (15:1+), position-scaledPick one horse listed at 15:1+ on the latest morning line. Payoff scales by where they finish. If your picked horse's odds tighten below 15:1 before lock and you don't update, the bet voids ($0) — players get an SMS when this happens.stake × odds × scale + stake (1st = full odds, 2nd = ½, 3rd = ⅓, outside top 3 OR no longer a longshot = $0)

Each payoff returns your stake plus the multiplied profit. Miss the bet → $0 (lose the stake). Odds are pari-mutuel: payouts use the odds at race time (last imported), not when you locked your pick. Same as the actual track.

Top 5 scoring detail (the gradient bet)

The Top 5 bet rewards both spread (more correct horses = bigger multiplier) and conviction on longshots (a 25/1 horse hitting adds 25 to the multiplier, vs. 5 for a 5/1).

multiplier = (# correct picks) + (sum of decimal odds of correct picks)

Example at $1,000 stake, actual top 5 = [#19, #1, #22, #12, #6]:

3 of 55/1, 25/1, 20/13 + 50 = 53$54,000
5 of 5full mix5 + sum of all 5often $50K–$100K+

3. Tiebreaker cascade

If two or more players tie on final bankroll, the system runs through these in order until one wins:

  1. Total $ staked on winning bets — whoever put more money on the bets that hit wins. Rewards conviction sizing.
  2. Trifecta position error (lowest) — sum of |slot − actual_finish_position| across your 3 trifecta picks. Lower error = closer to the actual finish = wins.
  3. Numeric tiebreaker (closest guess) — sum of |your guess − actual| across all tiebreaker questions (e.g., winning jockey's age). Lower = closer = wins. No stake, no payoff — just for breaking ties.
  4. Split the pot or coin flip — if still tied after all three layers above, admin makes the call. Extremely rare in practice.

5. Audit seal

Just before the race lock, the admin "seals" all entries. We compute a SHA-256 hash of the entry snapshot (canonical JSON, sorted keys, no timestamps) and publish it. Anyone can click Verify integrity on the event page to re-fetch the snapshot, recompute the hash, and confirm nothing was changed after seal time.

If picks were tampered with after sealing, the hash wouldn't match — verify would fail. The proof requires no trust in the platform or the admin.
