How the Math Works

Everything that decides a pool — payouts, parlay bonuses, and tiebreakers — is published here so the standings are fully auditable. ← Back

1. Payouts per pick

Each pick has a fixed stake (set per pool, e.g. $1,000 fun money). Payoff depends on the pick type:

Pick typePayoff if hitExample at $1,000 stake
Win / Place / Showstake × decimal odds (morning line)$1,000 on a 6/1 horse → $6,000
Trifecta (1-2-3 in order)50 × stake$50,000 if exact
Top-3 Box (any order)10 × stake$10,000 if all 3 in top 3
Longshot top-35 × stake$5,000 bonus
Over/Under, Yes/No propseven money (1 × stake)$1,000

Odds are pari-mutuel — payouts use the odds at race time (last imported), not when you locked in your pick. Same as the actual track.

2. Parlay bonus

You can lock 2 or 3 of your single-horse picks (Win, Place, or Show) into a parlay. If all locked picks hit:

bonus = stake × (odds₁ × odds₂ × odds₃)

Example: lock Win at 8/1 and Place at 5/2 with $1,000 stake. Both hit → bonus = 1,000 × 8 × 2.5 = $20,000. Miss any leg and the bonus is zero (individual picks still pay normally).

3. Bankroll = total winnings

Add up payouts across all picks that hit, plus any parlay bonus. That's your bankroll. Highest bankroll wins the pool.

4. Tiebreaker ladder

If two or more players tie on bankroll, the tiebreaker cascade kicks in. Each tier resolves first; if still tied, falls through to the next tier.

Tier 1 — Trifecta position error (lowest wins)

For each of your 3 trifecta picks: error = |slot − actual_finish_position|. Slot is 1, 2, or 3 (where you put the horse). Actual finish position is 1 through N (the whole field). Scratched/DNF horses get a 20-point penalty. Sum the three errors.

Tier 2 — Exact position hits (most wins)

Count how many of your 3 trifecta picks finished in the exact position you predicted. Range 0–3.

Tier 3 — Exacta error (lowest wins)

Same calculation as Tier 1 but only for your first two trifecta picks. Tighter test of who came closer on the top-2 finishers.

Tier 4 — Coin flip

If still tied after all three tiers, the system surfaces a coin flip — the admin makes a manual call.

6. Audit seal

Just before the race lock, the admin "seals" all entries. We compute a SHA-256 hash of the entry snapshot (canonical JSON, sorted keys, no timestamps) and publish it. Anyone can click Verify integrity on the event page to re-fetch the snapshot, recompute the hash, and confirm nothing was changed after seal time.

If picks were tampered with after sealing, the hash wouldn't match — verify would fail. The proof requires no trust in the platform or the admin.
